### 1. DERMACEUTIC
**Tagline:** Pharmaceutical-grade skincare. Clinical results. Visible transformation.

**Origin:** Founded in France, 1999. Developed by pharmacists and dermatologists inside a pharmaceutical laboratory — not a cosmetics company. This distinction matters.

**The Science:**
Dermaceutic specialises in high-concentration active ingredients that function at a pharmaceutical level. Their signature approach combines exfoliating acids (AHAs, TCA), vitamin C in clinically effective concentrations, retinol, and targeted brightening actives. Many of their products are used as home-maintenance protocols between in-clinic peels and treatments.

**The Customer:**
- Age: 30–55
- Skin concerns: Stubborn pigmentation, melasma, post-acne scarring, uneven skin tone, sun damage, loss of radiance
- Profile: Someone who has already tried over-the-counter skincare and found it insufficient. They want clinical results at home. They may already be receiving in-clinic treatments and need a home-care regime that works at the same level.
- Lifestyle: Skin-conscious, research-oriented, willing to invest in results

**Key Ranges:**
- **Milk Peel** — Gentle AHA resurfacing, entry point for new users of actives
- **C25 Cream** — 25% stabilised Vitamin C, for brightening and antioxidant protection
- **Spot Cream** — Targeted depigmentation with kojic acid, azelaic acid, and retinol
- **Retinol Cream** — Prescription-strength equivalent retinol for renewal
- **K Ceutic** — Post-procedure repair and maintenance

**When to recommend Dermaceutic:**
Pigmentation is the primary complaint. Or the customer is maintaining results from an in-clinic peel or laser treatment and needs pharmaceutical-grade home care to extend and protect results.

**When NOT to recommend:**
Active skin inflammation, rosacea, very sensitive skin, pregnancy (retinol-containing products).

### 2. HELIOCARE
**Tagline:** 360° sun protection. Inside and out.

**Origin:** Developed by Cantabria Labs in Spain. Built around a patented ingredient, Fernblock® — an extract of the Polypodium leucotomos fern, clinically proven to protect skin from UV damage both topically and when taken orally.

**The Science:**
Heliocare's uniqueness is twofold. First, their sunscreens go beyond SPF — they combine UVA + UVB + visible light + infrared protection, which is critical for preventing pigmentation in darker skin tones (Fitzpatrick III–VI) where visible light plays a significant role in melanin stimulation. Second, they offer oral photoprotection capsules that provide systemic UV protection from within — a concept proven in multiple peer-reviewed clinical trials.

**The Customer:**
- Age: All ages (teens through 65+)
- Skin concerns: Sun damage, pigmentation prevention, maintaining an even skin tone, general skin health
- Profile: Anyone living in a high-UV climate (South Africa's UV index makes this relevant to *every* patient). Particularly important for darker skin tones where pigmentation triggered by visible light is often the #1 concern.
- Lifestyle: Outdoor workers, athletes, golfers, anyone who spends time in the SA sun

**Key Ranges:**
- **Heliocare 360° Fluid** — Daily SPF 50+ for normal/combination skin
- **Heliocare 360° Gel Oil-Free** — Matte finish, ideal for acne-prone/oily skin
- **Heliocare 360° Pigment Solution** — SPF 50+ with active brighteners, for pigmentation-prone skin
- **Heliocare Oral Capsules** — Daily supplement for systemic photoprotection
- **Heliocare Pediatrics** — Sun protection formulated for children

**When to recommend Heliocare:**
Always — as a standalone brand or as the SPF layer in any multi-brand regime. No skincare routine is complete without sun protection, and Heliocare is the most clinically advanced SPF brand we carry. It is especially important for:
- Darker skin tones (where visible light drives pigmentation)
- Patients on active ingredients (retinol, AHAs, vitamin C) which increase photosensitivity
- Post-treatment skin (after peels, laser, IPL)

**When NOT to recommend as the only brand:**
It treats no active skin condition — it prevents and protects. It should almost always be combined with a treatment brand.

### 3. ISDIN
**Tagline:** Dermatologist-developed. Science with soul.

**Origin:** Founded in Barcelona, 1975. One of Spain's leading dermatological companies. ISDIN bridges the gap between pharmaceutical-grade efficacy and everyday usability — their products are clinically developed but designed to be gentle enough for daily use and sensible skincare routines.

**The Science:**
ISDIN's standout innovation is in sun care: their Eryfotona range contains DNA Repairsomes — enzymes (photolyase) that actually repair UV-damaged DNA in skin cells after sun exposure. This is not sun protection after the fact — it is DNA repair, which no other brand in our range offers. Their acne range (Acniben) is dermatologist-formulated for teens without the harshness that often comes with acne products.

**The Customer:**
- Age: 14–45 (the broadest age range of all 6 brands)
- Skin concerns: Acne, sensitivity, early ageing, daily sun care, body skin health
- Profile: A teenager with breakouts. A young adult building their first proper routine. A sensitive-skin patient who has reacted to stronger brands. A mother looking for products safe for her teenage children.
- Lifestyle: Practical, busy, wants to do the right thing without overcomplicating it

**Key Ranges:**
- **Acniben** — Teen-specific acne control: cleanser, spot gel, mattifying moisturiser
- **Eryfotona AK-NMSC** — DNA repair sunscreen (also used in actinic keratosis prevention)
- **Eryfotona Ageless** — Anti-aging SPF with DNA repair enzymes and hyaluronic acid
- **Isdinceutics** — Serums and actives: Hyaluronic Concentrate, Melatonik (overnight recovery), Vitamin C serum
- **Lambdapil** — Hair loss supplements for thinning/shedding hair

**When to recommend ISDIN:**
First-time skincare buyer of any age. Teen with acne. Sensitive skin that can't tolerate stronger actives. Patient who wants a simple, clean, effective routine. Anyone specifically asking about DNA repair or next-generation sun protection.

**When NOT to recommend:**
Severe, cystic acne (needs Roaccutane + dermatologist). Advanced anti-aging (stronger brands like Mesoestetic or SkinCeuticals will deliver more). Post-procedure skin needing pharmaceutical-grade repair (Dermaceutic).

### 4. MESOESTETIC
**Tagline:** Aesthetic medicine cosmeceuticals. Professional results. No compromise.

**Origin:** Founded in Barcelona, 1984. Mesoestetic began as a brand for aesthetic medicine professionals — their products were designed to be used alongside in-clinic treatments like mesotherapy, chemical peels, and lasers. They are now available for home use but carry the same professional-grade philosophy.

**The Science:**
Mesoestetic operates at the cosmeceutical level — the boundary between cosmetics and pharmaceuticals. Their formulas contain active ingredients in concentrations and combinations that are closer to what you would find in a medical treatment than a cosmetic product. Key technologies include their depigmentation complex (used in the globally recognised Cosmelan and Dermamelan protocols), stem cell activation for anti-aging, and their Age Element technology which targets multiple signs of aging simultaneously.

**The Customer:**
- Age: 35–60
- Skin concerns: Serious anti-aging, deep pigmentation and melasma, loss of firmness and volume, post-procedure maintenance
- Profile: A clinic patient who wants their home-care to match the quality of their in-clinic treatments. Result-driven. Has likely already tried other brands and wants more. Often already on a Cosmelan or Dermamelan depigmentation protocol through Star Aesthetic.
- Lifestyle: Professional, appearance-conscious, views skincare as an investment

**Key Ranges:**
- **Cosmelan Home** — Maintenance cream for patients who have completed the Cosmelan depigmentation treatment in-clinic
- **Age Element** — Premium anti-aging range: brightening elixir, anti-wrinkle ampoules, regenerating night cream
- **Hydra Vital** — Intensive hydration for dehydrated or post-treatment skin
- **Acne One** — Professional-grade acne control for adult hormonal acne
- **Stem Cell** — Growth factor-based regeneration for advanced anti-aging
- **Postcure** — Post-procedure recovery and healing

**When to recommend Mesoestetic:**
The customer has tried other skincare and wants the next level. They are receiving or planning in-clinic treatments. They have melasma or deep pigmentation. They are 40+ and want serious anti-aging results. Post-treatment home care after any Star Aesthetic clinical procedure.

**When NOT to recommend:**
New to skincare — the products require understanding and commitment. Teenagers. Budget-sensitive buyer (this is the premium tier of our range).

### 5. NEOSTRATA
**Tagline:** The science of skin renewal. Clinically proven AHA and PHA technology.

**Origin:** American brand founded by two dermatologists, Drs. Eugene Van Scott and Ruey Yu — the scientists who first published research proving the effectiveness of alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) on skin in the 1970s. NeoStrata *invented* the category of AHA skincare.

**The Science:**
NeoStrata's foundation is AHA (alpha hydroxy acid — glycolic, lactic) and PHA (polyhydroxy acid — gluconolactone, lactobionic acid) technology. AHAs exfoliate the skin surface, accelerate cell turnover, and stimulate collagen production. PHAs do the same but are larger molecules — they work more slowly and gently, making them ideal for sensitive skin. Their Skin Active range adds peptides, antioxidants, and retinol to create a comprehensive anti-aging system.

**The Customer:**
- Age: 25–55
- Skin concerns: Uneven skin texture, enlarged pores, acne (including post-acne marks), early to moderate signs of aging, dull and congested skin
- Profile: The person whose skin just never looks "fresh" — texture issues, occasional breakouts, skin that looks tired. They want a brand that will genuinely resurface and renew their skin, not just moisturise it.
- Lifestyle: Consistent, willing to follow a routine, understands that results take 6–8 weeks

**Key Ranges:**
- **Skin Active** — Premium anti-aging: Cellular Restoration cream, Intensive Eye Therapy, HA Serum
- **Clarify** — Acne control: Foaming Glycolic Wash, Mandelic Clarifying Toner, Acne Spot Treatment
- **Enlighten** — Brightening and pigmentation: Pigment Controller, Illuminating Serum
- **Restore** — Sensitive skin: Moisturising Face Cream, Bionic Face Serum (PHA-based)
- **Correct** — Body skin: Glycolic body lotion, smoothing body cream

**When to recommend NeoStrata:**
Acne-prone skin, post-acne scarring, rough or congested texture, dull skin needing renewal, early aging in 25–40 age group. Also excellent for body skin concerns (keratosis pilaris, rough elbows and knees, body acne).

**When NOT to recommend:**
Active skin inflammation or broken skin (AHAs will sting and worsen). Very sensitive or rosacea-prone skin (consider the Restore PHA range instead, or switch to ISDIN). Pregnancy (some AHA products are fine, but requires careful selection).

### 6. SKINCEUTICALS
**Tagline:** Prevent. Correct. Protect.

**Origin:** Founded in the United States, 1994. Owned by L'Oréal Luxe but operates as an independent professional brand sold exclusively through medical aesthetic practices and dermatology clinics. The brand was built on the research of Dr. Sheldon Pinnell at Duke University — specifically his discovery of the optimal pH and concentration for vitamin C to be absorbed by skin.

**The Science:**
SkinCeuticals is the most antioxidant-focused brand in our range. Their intellectual property centres on the Duke University patent for stabilised vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid) formulation — their CE Ferulic serum is considered the gold standard vitamin C product globally. Beyond vitamin C, they focus on correcting accumulated skin damage, preventing new environmental damage, and restoring skin barrier function. Their formulas are used before and after surgical procedures in leading clinics worldwide.

**The Customer:**
- Age: 35–65
- Skin concerns: Advanced anti-aging, sun damage correction, loss of firmness, fine lines and wrinkles, skin that needs antioxidant protection due to urban/environmental exposure
- Profile: The premium buyer. Often a professional woman 40+ who is serious about her skin, reads research, and wants the best. She may be buying from a competitor clinic and needs to be shown why SkinCeuticals is worth the investment. She is likely already using some form of vitamin C — this is her upgrade.
- Lifestyle: Urban professional, high sun exposure or environmental stress, high disposable income, loyalty to brands that deliver

**Key Ranges:**
- **CE Ferulic** — The benchmark vitamin C serum: 15% vitamin C, vitamin E, ferulic acid. Morning antioxidant.
- **Phloretin CF** — Lighter vitamin C serum with phloretin, better suited to combination/oily skin
- **Retinol 0.3 / 0.5 / 1.0** — Progressive retinol range for cell renewal and anti-aging
- **Triple Lipid Restore 2:4:2** — Ceramide, cholesterol, and fatty acid formula to restore skin barrier
- **AGE Interrupter** — Targets the cross-linking of collagen (glycation) — advanced anti-aging
- **Blemish + Age Defense** — Salicylic + glycolic + dioic acid for adult acne with aging concerns
- **Physical Fusion UV Defense** — Tinted mineral SPF 50 for sensitive, post-procedure, or reactive skin

**When to recommend SkinCeuticals:**
The customer is 35+ and wants premium anti-aging. They mention antioxidants, vitamin C, or environmental protection. They are post-laser, post-peel, or post-surgery and need trusted repair. They are a high-value client and you want to establish a long-term relationship with a brand they will be loyal to.

**When NOT to recommend:**
Budget-sensitive buyer — SkinCeuticals is the most expensive brand we carry. Teen acne. New to skincare (the range assumes a baseline of skincare knowledge).

### STAGE 1 — PRIMARY CONCERN
*Purpose: Identify the main problem to solve. This is the strongest filter in the recommendation matrix.*

**Niki asks:**
> "What's the main thing bothering you about your skin right now? Pick the one that matters most to you."

**Options:**
- A — Acne and breakouts
- B — Dark spots or uneven skin tone (pigmentation)
- C — Signs of ageing — lines, wrinkles, loss of firmness
- D — Dull, rough, or congested skin (texture and radiance)
- E — Redness, sensitivity, or reactive skin
- F — Sun damage or I just want good sun protection
- G — I'm not sure — my skin just doesn't look right

*If G is selected:*
> "That's okay — let's figure it out together. Would you say your skin tends to be more problematic (breakouts, oiliness) or more ageing-related (dryness, lines, dullness)?"
> Options: More problematic / More ageing-related / A bit of both

---

### STAGE 2 — AGE
*Purpose: Narrows brand selection and specific product tier.*

**Niki asks:**
> "What's your age group? This helps me understand which products are most appropriate for your skin's current needs."

**Options:**
- A — Under 20
- B — 20–30
- C — 31–40
- D — 41–50
- E — 51–60
- F — Over 60

---

### STAGE 3 — SKIN TYPE
*Purpose: Determines formulation type (gel vs cream, oil-free vs rich).*

**Niki asks:**
> "How would you describe your skin? Don't worry if you're not 100% sure — pick the closest match."

**Options:**
- A — Oily: shiny by midday, enlarged pores, prone to breakouts
- B — Dry: tight feeling, sometimes flaky, rarely oily
- C — Combination: oily in the T-zone (forehead, nose, chin), normal or dry on cheeks
- D — Sensitive: reacts to products, redness, stinging, or flushing easily
- E — Normal: generally balanced, no major issues with oiliness or dryness

---

### STAGE 4 — SKIN TONE (FITZPATRICK SCALE)
*Purpose: Critical for pigmentation recommendations. Darker tones require different formulations and are more sensitive to some actives.*

**Niki asks:**
> "What's your natural skin tone? This affects which products are safe and most effective for you — especially for sun protection and pigmentation treatment."

**Options (with visual guide on screen):**
- A — Very light, burns easily, rarely tans (Type I–II)
- B — Light to medium, sometimes burns, tans gradually (Type III)
- C — Medium to olive, rarely burns, tans easily (Type IV)
- D — Brown skin tone, very rarely burns (Type V)
- E — Deep brown or dark skin, almost never burns (Type VI)

---

### STAGE 5 — SPECIFIC FOLLOW-UP (branches based on Stage 1 answer)

#### If primary concern = ACNE (A):
> "Just a couple more questions about your breakouts. Are they:"
- Mostly surface-level (blackheads, whiteheads, occasional pimples)
- Deeper and more painful (cysts, nodules under the skin)
- Hormonal — mainly around your jaw, chin, or before your period

> "And how long have you been dealing with breakouts?"
- It's a recent thing (under 6 months)
- On and off for years
- Pretty much my whole life since my teens

#### If primary concern = PIGMENTATION (B):
> "Can you tell me more about your dark spots?"
- Sun spots (mostly on cheeks, forehead, décolletage)
- Melasma (larger patches, often symmetrical, worse in summer)
- Post-acne marks (dark marks left after breakouts heal)
- Mixed — I have a few of these

#### If primary concern = AGEING (C):
> "What concerns you most about the signs of ageing?"
- Fine lines and wrinkles (forehead, around eyes, mouth)
- Loss of firmness and elasticity — skin feels less "lifted"
- Dullness and loss of that fresh, glowing look
- All of the above

#### If primary concern = TEXTURE/RADIANCE (D):
> "How would you describe the problem?"
- My skin looks dull and flat no matter what I do
- My pores look enlarged or my skin feels rough
- I get congestion and small bumps but not full breakouts
- My skin tone is uneven — some areas darker, some lighter

#### If primary concern = SENSITIVITY/REDNESS (E):
> "Does your skin flush or redden easily?"
- Yes, it's almost always a bit red
- Only when I use certain products or in cold/hot weather
- I've been told I might have rosacea

#### If primary concern = SUN PROTECTION (F):
> No follow-up needed — proceed to Stage 6.

- Mostly indoors (office, home)
- Moderate — some time outside daily
- High — I'm outdoors a lot (sport, outdoor work, regular beach/pool time)

**Current skincare:**
> "What does your current skincare routine look like?"
- Nothing really — soap and water, maybe a basic moisturiser
- A few basics — cleanser, moisturiser, SPF
- A full routine with active ingredients (serums, acids, retinol)

**Medications (critical for safety):**
> "Are you currently using or taking any of the following? Please select all that apply."
- Roaccutane (isotretinoin) — oral acne medication
- A prescription retinol or retinoid cream
- Antibiotics (oral or topical) for acne
- No, none of the above

**Pregnancy / Breastfeeding:**

- Yes
- No

## PART 4 — RECOMMENDATION LOGIC MATRIX

This matrix maps inputs to outputs. It is the engine behind Niki's recommendations.

### PRIMARY FILTERS (applied in order):

#### Safety overrides (applied FIRST — non-negotiable):
| Condition | Products excluded |
|---|---|
| Pregnant or breastfeeding | Retinol (all brands), high-dose salicylic acid, Cosmelan, Dermamelan |
| On Roaccutane | All AHAs, all exfoliating acids, all retinol, Dermaceutic peels |
| On prescription retinoid | All other retinol products (duplication risk) |
| Active skin inflammation | AHAs, retinol, vitamin C at high concentration |
| Cystic acne | Do NOT recommend home-care as primary solution — refer to Dr. Bangalee |

#### Brand routing by primary concern + age:

| Primary Concern | Under 20 | 20–30 | 31–40 | 41–50 | 51+ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Acne | ISDIN Acniben | ISDIN / NeoStrata Clarify | NeoStrata / SkinCeuticals B+A | Mesoestetic Acne One | Mesoestetic |
| Pigmentation | Heliocare + ISDIN | Heliocare + NeoStrata Enlighten | Heliocare + Dermaceutic | Heliocare + Mesoestetic | Heliocare + Mesoestetic/Dermaceutic |
| Anti-aging | — (too young) | NeoStrata Skin Active | NeoStrata / SkinCeuticals | SkinCeuticals / Mesoestetic | Mesoestetic Age Element / SkinCeuticals |
| Texture/Radiance | NeoStrata Clarify | NeoStrata | NeoStrata / Dermaceutic | Dermaceutic / SkinCeuticals | SkinCeuticals / Mesoestetic |
| Sensitivity | ISDIN | ISDIN / NeoStrata Restore | NeoStrata Restore / SkinCeuticals | SkinCeuticals Triple Lipid | SkinCeuticals Triple Lipid |
| Sun Protection | Heliocare | Heliocare | Heliocare | Heliocare | Heliocare |

#### Skin type modifiers:
| Skin Type | Formulation preference |
|---|---|
| Oily | Gel, oil-free, matte finish formulations |
| Dry | Rich creams, oils, barrier-restoring formulations |
| Combination | Gel moisturiser, balancing SPF, lightweight serums |
| Sensitive | PHAs over AHAs, fragrance-free, mineral SPF |
| Normal | Any — guide by concern and brand fit |

#### Skin tone modifiers (Fitzpatrick):
| Tone | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Type I–II | Standard recommendations apply |
| Type III | Add Heliocare 360° Pigment Solution as SPF regardless of concern |
| Type IV–VI | Heliocare 360° Pigment Solution mandatory. Avoid high-strength AHAs initially. Emphasise post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation risk of all actives. Prefer azelaic acid, tranexamic acid, kojic acid-based brighteners. |

### STARTER REGIME FORMAT (always recommend in this order):

1. **Cleanser** — morning and evening
2. **Treatment serum or active** — the core corrective product
3. **Moisturiser** — protection and hydration
4. **SPF** — always Heliocare as the sun protection layer
5. **Optional: Eye cream** — if 35+ or specific eye concern
6. **Optional: Night treatment** — retinol or overnight mask
